School for Good and Evil

This series didn't really impact me in any grandiose way. It's a twist on the fairy tale trope where the children of the big heroes/heroines attend either the School for Good, or the School for Evil, and learn how to be a proper villain/hero. There's nothing bad about it, per se, I think it just felt very juvenile. If I had read this book when I was younger, I would've loved it. As an adult, however, it just feels surface level- a lot of the morals and lessons are very much obvious, like, don't judge people based on appearances. Basic stuff. Regardless, it's a nice, light read if you're just looking to kill time.


Final Rating: ✨✨✨✨/5

The Sun and The Star

This one. Honestly, I would put it in Level 3, but out of loyalty to Rick, I must give it some leniency. It's about Will and Nico journeying through Tartarus to rescue their friend Bob, the Titan. I just found it to be very juvenile, again. The writing from the previous books was drastically better compared to this one. It was more nuanced and mature. This felt like something I'd read in fifth grade. I suppose his target audience is that range, so it makes sense. But still. A lot of the writing felt awkward and embarrassing, like they were trying too hard to sell the message of the power of love or whatever. It more-so focused on mental health and healing, which I do think is important for young children to read. I just think it could have been executed better.


Final Rating: ✨✨✨/5

Five Survive

Five Survive was as good as a murder mystery can be. It's about a group of friends who get trapped in an RV while road-tripping. They find out they've been ambushed by a rogue, mystery man, and they have to figure out who it is, and what they want. Otherwise, they die. It was one of the better ones, in that I didn't immediately guess who was the suspect, but I found the writing to be clunky in the beginning. It fell into a flow nicely later on, though. It was predictable, but I think that most YA murder mysteries are kind of predictable though, so this book isn't unique in that. However, the main character's quirks are very noticeable, and they're never fully explained. I still don't know why she did some of the shit she did. The ending though, oh man. It made me so emotional; it really focused on the bond between a mother and daughter. It was so sweet, it made up for the mediocrity of the rest of it.

Final Rating: ✨✨✨✨/5
