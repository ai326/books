It All Comes Back To You

I don't think I've despised a book as much as I despise this one. The main character, Kiran's, sister is getting married to the brother of the guy she hates, Deen. This detail is irrelevant, mostly, because Kiran wants to break up the happy couple. Why? Because A) Faisal, (the brother) was previously a drug addict, (KEY WORD PREVIOUS), except Kiran thinks that he's not good enough for her sister, Amira. I understand why that could make her wary, but man, everyone has issues. And the fact that he worked through them, and is clearly trying every day to be good enough for Amira shows that he's not that same guy anymore. In fact, he is quite possibly the most gentle, loving man I've ever seen, and is clearly pathetically in love with her sister. So, to have Kiran immediately discount him because of his issues in the past honestly reflects more on her character than his. B) Kiran is extremely traumatized. Her mother died a few years ago, so now she's determined to keep her family together. She does this the only way she knows how, by preventing their marriage. I do pity her on this, because I can't imagine having to go through that trauma. I mean, it's just absolutely horrible. BUT. This does not give her a free pass to be a jerk! I think that's what irked me the most; Kiran just dumping her trauma on everyone else, even though everyone in this book has their own issues, but no one tries to sabotage anyone else because of it. What makes Kiran special? And the fact that there was no redemption arc, even after she royally screwed everything up? Nah. This book is truly the worst.

You Will Be Mine

This book isn't bad, I just think it tried too hard to be unpredictable. It's about Lylah and her group of friends trying to unwind after a stressful few weeks at school. They end up going out to this party, but just as they're about to leave, one of the friends, Sonny, gets an anonymous letter from a secret admirer saying some creepy shit. They laugh it off, but turns out, Sonny never comes home. He's dead. Now, this seemed like the perfect set up for a nice, freaky mystery, but the execution just felt off. First of all, the author kept making it incredibly obvious as to who the killer IS NOT by making it seem like it is that person, if that makes sense? She kept emphasizing that it's probably this one, random guy, with no motive whatsoever. It just made it incredibly obvious that it wasn't him. I understand that's a pretty normal technique in mystery novels, but it was just overdone here. Also, for no particular reason, I found the main character to be extremely whiny and annoying and 'oh someone, please protect me'. She just seemed overly ditzy for no particular reason. I also found the actual culprit to be more laughable than scary. I didn't sympathize with him, which I suppose is good, but I think the most well-written villains are the ones you can feel for. I just didn't get that from this. All in all, it just fell flat.
